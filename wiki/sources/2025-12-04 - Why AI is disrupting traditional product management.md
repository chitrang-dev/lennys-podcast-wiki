# Why AI is disrupting traditional product management

## Summary

Tomer Cohen describes how LinkedIn approached product turnaround work, why clarity matters more than being right, and how AI-first teams should be organized around objectives rather than legacy structures.

## Key Points

- "We might be wrong, but we are not confused" is a leadership principle centered on clarity of thought and execution.
- Teams should define the exact problem, audience, and tradeoffs before debating solutions.
- Execution quality depends on resourcing and talent allocation matching declared priorities.
- LinkedIn's feed turnaround required a dedicated team, AI-first integration, and a protected user cohort for experimentation.
- Product ambition should be set from potential and mission, not only current metrics.

## Notable Examples

- LinkedIn isolated roughly two million members into a protected cohort to test a redesigned feed without constant organizational interference.
- The feed strategy shifted from an activity or promotional feed to a professional knowledge-exchange product.
- AI objectives evolved toward better downstream conversations rather than simple click optimization.
