# Persuasive communication and managing up

## Summary

Wes Kao lays out practical communication frameworks for gaining buy-in, being concise, and improving the quality of written and verbal communication in leadership settings.

## Key Points

- Communication problems often reflect insufficient clarity or framing from the speaker, not just inattentive listeners.
- Strong communicators practice consistently, not only in high-stakes executive settings.
- Sell first, then explain logistics, especially when buy-in is not yet secure.
- Concision is about density and clarity, not merely reducing word count.
- Preparation and anticipation of obvious objections improve both meetings and writing.

## Frameworks

- `MOO`: Most Obvious Objection. Anticipate the likely pushback before presenting.
- `Sales, then logistics`: Create motivation and context before process detail.
- `Play like it's game day`: Use everyday interactions as practice for executive communication.
