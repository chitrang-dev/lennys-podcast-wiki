# AI UX and Interaction Design

## Summary

The AI product conversations make it clear that interface design has not disappeared. It has become more ambiguous and more important. Teams now have to design conversations, control surfaces, confidence cues, and moments of human override rather than just screens and flows.

## Core Ideas

- NLX is not the absence of design. It is a new interaction grammar.
- Trust and usability are deeply linked in AI interfaces.
- Human control must often be visible, not merely theoretically available.
- Great AI UX translates fuzzy capability into legible, useful behavior.

## Key Syntheses

- `NLX is the new UX`
  Aparna Chennapragada argues that conversational and natural-language interfaces still require structure, grammar, and product craft.
- `Interaction design is where agency gets domesticated`
  AI capability may be broad, but interface design determines how safely and effectively people can use it.
- `Reliability needs UX expression`
  Confidence, citation, fallback, and override patterns help users understand what the system is doing and when to trust it.
- `AI products need stronger taste, not weaker taste`
  Several guests push back on the idea that models make product craft less important.

## What Good Looks Like

- The interface makes system behavior legible.
- Users know what the product is good at and where it may fail.
- There are clear escape hatches or human-control moments where needed.
- Conversation and UI elements work together rather than compete.

## Failure Modes

- Treating chat as a complete product strategy.
- Hiding important uncertainty behind confident outputs.
- Assuming natural language removes the need for product design.
- Creating interfaces that feel magical in demos but brittle in repeated use.
