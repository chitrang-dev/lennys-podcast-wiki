# AI Product Development Lifecycle

## Summary

The clearest pattern across the AI product episodes is that AI products need a different development lifecycle than traditional software. Teams have to work with non-determinism, shifting capabilities, and trust-sensitive workflows, which changes how they scope, prototype, ship, and learn.

## Core Ideas

- Start from the problem and workflow, not the model.
- Begin with a narrow use case that forces learning.
- Prototype earlier and more often because the design space is still moving.
- Treat shipping as the beginning of calibration, not the end of implementation.

## Key Syntheses

- `Start small to learn faster`
  Aishwarya Reganti and Kiriti Badam emphasize step-by-step scope expansion because wide agentic systems create complexity before the team understands the problem.
- `Prototype is the new specification`
  Aparna Chennapragada argues that if you are not prototyping with AI, you are likely reasoning too abstractly about a fast-moving medium.
- `AI-first changes team structure`
  Tomer Cohen suggests AI cannot live as a detached service team; the product organization itself has to be reoriented around AI-enabled objectives.
- `Build near the frontier, but not blindly`
  Kevin Weil highlights that model capabilities are improving so quickly that teams should not over-anchor to current limitations.

## What Good Looks Like

- A meaningful but tightly-scoped first use case.
- Fast prototype loops tied to concrete user problems.
- Clear expansion logic from narrow workflow to broader system.
- Product, engineering, and leadership learning directly from usage.

## Failure Modes

- Starting with an agent architecture before understanding the user problem.
- Writing static plans for a medium that requires iterative discovery.
- Treating AI as a thin layer on top of old workflows.
- Overbuilding before trust and utility are proven.
