# Growth Loops and Word of Mouth

## Summary

Several of the most useful growth episodes shift the frame from isolated funnel optimization to compounding systems. The central question becomes: what user behavior creates more value, more distribution, or more learning as the product grows?

## Core Ideas

- The best growth systems compound.
- Recommendation is a stronger signal than incremental conversion alone.
- Loops outperform one-off tactics because they generate repeated returns.
- Word of mouth is often earned through unexpectedly strong product value, not by asking for referrals earlier or louder.

## Key Syntheses

- `Recommendation requires delight, not just efficiency`
  Nilan Peiris argues that customers talk when the product does something meaningfully better than they expected, not merely when the signup flow is smoother.
- `Loops are a better planning unit than tactics`
  Shishir Mehrotra's loop framing suggests growth work should be organized around recurring systems of value creation and propagation.
- `NPS and referral behavior can be operationalized`
  Wise's use of recommendation proxies and product pillars shows that word of mouth can be measured and designed for without becoming fluffy.
- `The best growth loops connect product and distribution`
  Strong loops often combine value delivery, retention, and discovery rather than living inside one team boundary.

## What Good Looks Like

- The team can name its primary growth loop.
- Recommendation or sharing emerges from real user value.
- Loop metrics connect to retention and business quality, not just top-of-funnel volume.
- Growth work improves the product, not only the acquisition layer.

## Failure Modes

- Treating referrals as a widget rather than an output of product quality.
- Running disconnected experiments without a loop hypothesis.
- Overvaluing conversion wins that do not improve downstream retention or recommendation.
- Confusing virality with durable loop quality.
