# Clarity and Alignment

## Summary

High-performing teams benefit from explicit problem framing, direct disagreement, and visible alignment between stated priorities and actual execution.

## Key Points

- Clarity of thought requires naming the exact problem being solved, not a vague category of work.
- Clarity of execution means staffing, resources, and priorities must match declared goals.
- Confusion is often more damaging than being wrong because it fragments effort and weakens learning.
- Clear communication reduces avoidable skepticism and operational drag.
