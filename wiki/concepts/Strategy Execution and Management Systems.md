# Strategy Execution and Management Systems

## Summary

The strategy material in this vault does not treat execution as a separate downstream topic. A strategy is only real when management systems, capabilities, resourcing, and communication reinforce it.

## Core Ideas

- Strategy without systems is aspiration.
- Management systems are part of the strategy, not after-the-fact implementation detail.
- Strategy needs visible translation into roadmaps, ownership, and cadence.
- Misalignment usually shows up in resource allocation before it shows up in slide decks.

## Key Syntheses

- `Capabilities and systems are strategic choices`
  Roger Martin explicitly includes required capabilities and enabling management systems in the strategy choice cascade.
- `Execution reveals the true priority stack`
  Tomer Cohen's point about resourcing and top talent allocation is a direct test of whether the stated strategy is real.
- `The roadmap should reflect the strategy`
  Multiple guests push against treating the roadmap itself as the strategy.
- `Operating systems preserve coherence`
  Claire Hughes Johnson and other leadership guests reinforce that cadence, decision rights, and planning systems are the bridge from strategic intent to consistent action.

## What Good Looks Like

- Resource allocation matches stated priorities.
- The roadmap reflects strategic logic.
- Teams know how current work ladders up.
- The organization has the capabilities needed for its chosen path.

## Failure Modes

- Declaring priorities that do not match staffing or incentives.
- Treating management systems as neutral plumbing.
- Letting local team optimization overpower company strategy.
- Writing strategy docs that never materially change execution.
