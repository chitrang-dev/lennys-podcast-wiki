# Winning Strategy - 5 Questions

Use this template to turn a fuzzy strategic ambition into a coherent set of choices. It is based on Roger Martin's strategy cascade: winning aspiration, where to play, how to win, required capabilities, and enabling management systems.

## 1. Winning Aspiration

What does winning mean in this context?

- What outcome are we trying to create?
- For whom are we trying to create it?
- What would success look like in 2-3 years?
- What are we explicitly not trying to optimize for?

## 2. Where To Play

Where will we compete?

- Which customer or user segment matters most?
- Which use case or problem space are we prioritizing?
- Which geography, channel, price point, or product surface matters?
- What adjacent spaces are we intentionally excluding for now?

Draft:

## 3. How To Win

Why will customers choose us there?

- What is our core advantage logic?
- Will we win through differentiation, cost, speed, trust, convenience, ecosystem leverage, or some other mechanism?
- What would make the alternative less compelling?
- Why is this win logic believable rather than aspirational?

Draft:

## 4. Required Capabilities

What must we become especially good at to deliver the win logic?

- Which product, go-to-market, operational, technical, or brand capabilities matter most?
- Which of these capabilities are already strong?
- Which are missing or weak?
- Which capability gaps would break the strategy if left unresolved?

Draft:

## 5. Enabling Management Systems

What systems must exist to support the capabilities?

- What hiring profile, org design, or decision rights are required?
- What planning cadence, budgeting logic, metrics, or incentives are needed?
- What operating rituals or review mechanisms should reinforce the strategy?
- What current management habits are misaligned with the strategy?

Draft:

## Fit Check

Pressure-test the full chain.

- Do the five answers reinforce each other?
- Is the where-to-play narrow enough to create focus?
- Is the how-to-win specific enough to be tested?
- Do the capabilities clearly support the win logic?
- Do the management systems make the capabilities repeatable?
- What breaks if a competitor copies one visible piece of the strategy?
